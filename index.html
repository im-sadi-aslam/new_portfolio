Curriculum Design

Curriculum design involves planning educational content, learning experiences, and assessments that meet learners' needs and educational goals. Effective curriculum design ensures a well-rounded and engaging learning experience.

Pedagogical Approaches

Constructivist Learning

Students actively construct their own understanding and knowledge of the world through experiences and reflecting on those experiences. I encourage exploration, critical thinking, and discussion in the classroom.

Differentiated Instruction

Every learner is unique. I adapt my teaching methods to suit varied learning styles, abilities, and interests, ensuring each student receives personalized support.

Inquiry-Based Learning

My lessons promote curiosity and investigative learning where students pose questions, research, and share findings‚Äîleading to deeper understanding and engagement.

Project-Based Learning

Real-world problems and collaborative projects form the core of my approach. This develops problem-solving skills, teamwork, and application of knowledge.

Use of Educational Technology

I integrate smart tools like Google Classroom, Kahoot, Padlet, and digital presentations to enhance interactivity, engagement, and accessibility in learning.

Diagnostic Assessment

Used before instruction begins, diagnostic assessments identify students' prior knowledge, skills, and learning gaps. This helps tailor teaching to individual needs.

  • Pre-tests on grammar and vocabulary
  • KWL charts (What students Know, Want to know, and Learned)
  • Initial reading comprehension quizzes

Formative Assessment

Ongoing assessments during instruction that provide feedback for both teacher and student to guide learning progress.

  • Exit tickets at the end of lessons
  • Think-Pair-Share activities
  • Quizzes and oral questioning

Summative Assessment

Evaluates student learning at the end of an instructional unit by comparing it against some standard or benchmark.

  • Final exams or unit tests
  • Project presentations
  • Written assignments or research papers

Self and Peer Assessment

Encourages students to reflect on their learning and evaluate themselves or their peers using structured criteria, fostering responsibility and metacognitive skills.

  • Self-rating checklists after tasks
  • Peer reviews of presentations
  • Collaborative rubrics

Digital Assessment Tools

Utilizing technology to assess student learning in engaging, real-time, and data-driven ways.

  • Kahoot quizzes for instant feedback
  • Google Forms assessments with auto-grading
  • Padlet for reflective journals or idea boards
